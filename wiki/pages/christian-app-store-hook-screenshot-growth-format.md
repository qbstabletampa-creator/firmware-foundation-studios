## Notes

**Capture type:** Content seed, Pattern, Reference, Project context

Source post from Adrià Martinez:

> Stop overthinking your marketing. Try this instead. Slide 1: a hook. Slide 2: a photo of your app.

The quoted article preview says the system uses multiple AI faces, each with its own account, and ships three slideshows per day pointing back to an app.

The useful part for FFS is not the AI influencer spam angle. The useful part is the simple repeatable format:

1. Say one parent or kid benefit clearly.
2. Show the actual product.
3. Point to one simple next step.

## Why It Matters

The Christian App Store needs a clean way to show parents the value fast.

This format can market the store without long videos, heavy edits, or overexplaining the mission. The second slide should do the proof work: real game screen, game listing, parent safe promise, giveback page, no ads message, or App Store screenshot.

The bigger idea is a repeatable launch lane for every game in the factory.

## Guardrails

- Do not use fake AI influencer accounts as the public FFS strategy.
- Do not call the games addictive in parent facing copy.
- Use real game screens and real parent trust points.
- Keep the hook warm, clear, and parent trusted.
- Avoid shame based faith copy. Make it feel like fun first, with Bible truth inside the loop.
